Ray Dalio: Investor, Hedge Fund Manager, Philanthropist

Ray Dalio is one of the people who has been in the list of the most influential people in the world for the past three years. This is due to his continued efforts to ensure that his hedge fund, Bridgewater Associates (the world's biggest hedge fund firm), remain at the top.

  • 1949 - Born August 1, in Jackson Heights, New York City.
  • 1960 – Caddied at the Links Golf Club in New York. Here he listened to the conversations of his nigh net worth clients he caddied for speak about the wonders of the stock market.
  • 1961 - Aged 12, bought his first shares of Northeast Airlines and tripled his investment when the airline merged with another company.
  • 1973 - Commodities trader at Merrill Lynch.
  • 1974 – Earned an MBA from Harvard Business School.
  • 1975 – Aged 26, he founded Bridgewater Associates in his 2-bedroom Manhattan Apartment.

    • Today Bridgewater is the 5th most important private company in the U.S.

  • 1976 – Married his wife of today, Barbara. They have four sons together.
  • 2001 – Opened flagship hedge fund, Pure Alpha. (continues to still be successful even though the 2008 financial crisis.
  • 2007 - He met with Treasury and White House officials to warn them of the impending financial crisis, but no one listened
  • 2012 – Under Ray Dalio’s leadership and guidance, Bridgewater Associates became the biggest hedge fund in the world, with over $160 billion in assets under management.
  • 2013 – Received the Manager Lifetime Achievement Award.
  • 2018
    • - Working to make sure Bridgewater survives him, Dalio moved to turn Bridgewater into a partnership and gave employees more of a stake in the firm.
    • - Signed Warren Buffet’s Giving Pledge.
  • 2019 – Dalio’s net worth was $18.4 billion, making him one of the wealthiest self-made men in America.
